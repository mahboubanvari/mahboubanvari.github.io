About



My work focuses on the subtle poetry of ordinary life, capturing fleeting moments and quiet spaces that hold traces of memory, transition, and belonging. I explore the relationship between people and the spaces they inhabit, and how these spaces can carry identity, presence, and absence all at once.

Relocation and adaptation have deeply shaped my perspective. Moving from Iran to the United States two years ago, brought a new awareness of how environments define us—routines change, and spaces gradually become extensions of memory. Through this shift, I became aware of how new and unfamiliar places slowly gather their own sense of familiarity with time.

While my paintings are rooted in personal experience, I approach them openly, hoping that others might see or feel something of their own within the work.
